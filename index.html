Why Become Cabin Crew in Malaysia?

Malaysia's aviation industry is booming with world-class carriers like Malaysia Airlines, AirAsia, and Batik Air offering incredible opportunities. As a cabin crew member, you will enjoy competitive salaries (RM 3,500 to RM 12,000+), free travel perks, hotel stays, and the chance to explore the world while getting paid.

Beyond the glamour, you will develop life-saving skills, master crisis management, and build a career with endless growth potential — from In-Flight Supervisor to Cabin Crew Trainer or even transitioning to ground operations and corporate aviation roles.

Complete Career Guide

Everything you need to know to become a certified Cabin Crew member in Malaysia.

Basic Requirements

Citizenship & Age

Malaysian citizen aged between 18 to 35 years old. Some airlines accept up to 30 for females and 35 for males.

Height Requirements

Female: Minimum 157cm (barefoot). Male: Minimum 165cm (barefoot). Must reach emergency equipment overhead.

Weight & BMI

Weight must be proportionate to height (healthy BMI range). Airlines conduct BMI checks during medical exams.

Education

Minimum SPM with credits in English and Mathematics. Diploma or Degree holders have an advantage.

Health & Physical

Eyesight

Minimum 6/9 vision correctable to 6/6 with glasses or contact lenses. No color blindness. LASIK usually accepted after 6 months.

Tattoos & Scars

No visible tattoos when wearing uniform (short sleeves, knee-length skirt). No obvious scars on visible areas.

Dental & Appearance

Good dental hygiene with straight, clean teeth. Clear skin complexion. Professional appearance at all times.

Languages

Fluent English and Bahasa Malaysia mandatory. Additional languages (Mandarin, Japanese, Arabic) are major advantages.

Pro Tips for Meeting Requirements

  • Start working on your English proficiency early — take IELTS or MUET for certification
  • If you have tattoos, consider removal or placement that will not be visible in uniform
  • Maintain a fitness routine — airlines check BMI and physical stamina during water drills
  • Practice your smile and posture — first impressions during walk-in interviews matter enormously
  • Learn basic phrases in Mandarin, Japanese, or Korean to stand out from other candidates

Application Journey

Phase 1: Online Application / Walk-In

Submit your resume through the airline's career portal or attend walk-in interviews. Bring copies of certificates, passport photos, full-length photo in business attire, and a well-prepared CV highlighting customer service experience.

Phase 2: Initial Screening

Height and weight measurement, document verification, and a brief introduction round. Be confident, smile genuinely, and maintain eye contact. Dress professionally in business attire with subtle makeup.

Phase 3: Group Discussion / Debate

You will be grouped with other candidates to discuss a topic. Airlines assess teamwork, communication skills, and leadership. Do not dominate — facilitate, listen, and build on others' ideas.

Phase 4: One-on-One Interview

Behavioral questions about handling difficult customers, teamwork scenarios, and motivation for joining. Prepare STAR method answers (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Phase 5: Medical Examination

Comprehensive health check including vision, hearing, blood tests, urine tests, X-ray, ECG, and pregnancy test (for females). Must pass CAAM medical standards.

Phase 6: Training & Graduation

4-6 weeks of intensive training covering safety, first aid, service, and aircraft-specific procedures. Pass all exams and practical assessments to receive your Cabin Crew Attestation.

Female Grooming

Hair

Neatly tied in a bun or French twist. Hair must not touch the collar. Only natural black or dark brown colors. No flyaways — use hairspray and gel.

Makeup

Professional makeup mandatory: foundation, powder, blush, eyeliner, mascara, and red or coral lipstick. Nails must be manicured with clear, French, or airline-approved colors only.

Jewelry & Accessories

One pair of small stud earrings (max 1cm). One simple ring (wedding band allowed). No necklaces, bracelets, or ankle bracelets visible. Watch must be plain and professional.

Footwear & Uniform

Closed-toe black leather heels (3-5cm). Skin-colored stockings mandatory. Uniform must be pressed and immaculate. Scarves tied according to airline standard.

Male Grooming

Hair & Facial Hair

Neat, professional haircut. Sideburns not below earlobe. Clean-shaven or neatly trimmed mustache only. No beards or goatees for most Malaysian airlines.

Personal Hygiene

Clean, trimmed nails. Subtle cologne (not overpowering). Fresh breath — always carry mints. Well-maintained hands and skin.

Jewelry & Accessories

One ring only (wedding band). No earrings, bracelets, or necklaces. Simple professional watch. Tie must be properly knotted and at correct length.

Footwear & Uniform

Black leather oxford shoes, polished. Black socks only. Suit must be well-fitted and pressed. Belt must match shoe color. Tie clip optional but neat.

Training Modules

Comprehensive breakdown of what you will learn during cabin crew training in Malaysia.

Training Progress Overview 6 Core Modules

Safety & Emergency Procedures (SEP)

The most critical module covering aircraft evacuation (land and water), firefighting with actual fire drills, smoke-filled cabin procedures, door operation (including failure of power assist), slide descent from full height, and crowd control during emergencies. You will practice until muscle memory takes over.

Duration: 2 weeks | Pass Rate Required: 100%

First Aid & CPR (AED)

Comprehensive medical training including CPR, AED usage, handling choking passengers, treating burns, fractures, and medical emergencies like heart attacks or allergic reactions at 35,000 feet. You will be certified by recognized medical institutions.

Duration: 1 week | Recertify every 12 months

In-Flight Service & F&B

Wine and beverage knowledge, food service protocols, special meal handling (kosher, halal, vegan, gluten-free), tray setup, galley management, and premium cabin dining service. Includes bar cart service and duty-free sales procedures.

Duration: 1 week | Practical assessment included

Aircraft Familiarization

Detailed study of aircraft types operated by your airline — cabin layouts, emergency equipment locations, door types, slide/raft operations, oxygen systems, and communication equipment. You will memorize every exit and equipment location.

Duration: 1 week | Type-specific per aircraft

Communication & Customer Service

PA announcements in English and Bahasa Malaysia, handling difficult passengers, conflict resolution, cultural sensitivity, upselling techniques, and delivering the airline's brand of hospitality. Role-playing is a major component.

Duration: 3 days | Continuous assessment

Ditching & Water Survival

Swimming proficiency test, life jacket operation, life raft boarding and management, hypothermia prevention, sea survival signaling, and passenger management during water evacuation. Conducted at swimming pools and sometimes open water.

Duration: 2 days | Must pass swimming test

Important Training Notes

  • Training is physically demanding — expect long hours, early mornings, and physical drills
  • You must pass ALL modules with minimum scores (usually 80-90%) to graduate
  • Failure in Safety & Emergency Procedures means automatic dismissal — no second chances
  • Training is usually unpaid or with a small allowance — budget accordingly for 1-2 months
  • Upon graduation, you will receive a Cabin Crew Attestation recognized by CAAM (Civil Aviation Authority of Malaysia)
